## 1. Executive Summary
In response to EBI’s Request for Proposals, this document outlines a framework designed to bridge the gap between field-level expertise and high-frequency digital data. By integrating granular conflict metrics with longitudinal education data, we propose a system that empowers field staff to transition from reactive monitoring to proactive education continuity planning.

## 2. Context: The Conflict-Education Gap
Our preliminary analysis across 24 conflict-affected countries reveals that traditional metrics often mask the localized reality of school dropouts. While Gross Enrollment may appear stable, children are often "quietly" dropping out due to localized violence.

### Key Insights from Preliminary Research:
*   **Conflict vs. Persistence:** Sharp spikes in localized conflict (ACLED) correlate with immediate drops in "Survival Rate," even when enrollment remains stable.
*   **The "Vulnerability Clock":** Using proximity analysis, we can identify schools within 50km of recurring conflict and predict dropout trends before they appear in annual reports.
*   **Gender Sensitivity:** In escalating violence, female survival rates often decline more sharply, necessitating gender-specific emergency programming.

---

## 3. Strategic Phasing: Pilot, Build, Transfer
We propose a three-phase approach designed to ensure immediate value while building long-term organizational independence.

### Phase 1: Pilot (The Data Foundation)
We will begin by harmonizing EBI’s existing school registries with open-source ACLED and UNESCO metrics. This phase focuses on calculating the **Dynamic Vulnerability Index** for a select region (e.g., Lebanon or Sudan) to validate field-level intuition with hard data.
*   **Proof of Concept:** [Conflict vs. Education Survival Dashboard](https://rsnl42.github.io/CC1_lebanon/conflict_edu_analysis.html)

### Phase 2: Build (The Monitoring Infrastructure)
Once validated, we will build a scalable pipeline that automates the ingestion of fragmented reports. This phase introduces AI-assisted tools to clean and pivot disparate data sources into a unified format, powering an **Early Warning System**.
*   **Narrative Enabler:** [Interactive Vulnerability Timeline](https://rsnl42.github.io/CC1_lebanon/timeline_map/index.html) – Demonstrating our ability to track risk migration across borders over two decades.
### Phase 3: Transfer (Capacity & Sustainability)
The final phase focuses on hand-over. We will deliver a **Methodology Guide** and conduct training sessions for EBI field staff. The goal is to ensure the system is "indicator-agnostic"—allowing EBI to pivot the analysis to new metrics or countries without external support.
