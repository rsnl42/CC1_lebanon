# Request for Proposals (RFP): Strengthening Education Continuity in Conflict-Affected Regions

**Issued by:** Education Bridge Initiative (EBI)
**Date of Issue:** April 21st, 2026

## 1. Introduction and Background

The Education Bridge Initiative (EBI) works to ensure access to quality education for children and adolescents in regions affected by armed conflict and instability. Since 2014, EBI has operated in 12 countries across Sub-Saharan Africa, the Middle East, and South-East Asia, supporting over 800 schools through infrastructure rehabilitation, teacher training, learning material distribution, and emergency education programming.

In conflict-affected regions, education infrastructure faces compounding pressures. Schools may be damaged or destroyed, repurposed for military or shelter use, or rendered inaccessible by insecurity. Even where buildings remain intact, enrolment drops sharply when families are displaced or when the route to school becomes unsafe. The consequences extend well beyond the classroom: children out of school are more vulnerable to recruitment by armed groups, early marriage, and long-term economic exclusion.

EBI’s field teams have developed strong relationships with local education authorities and community leaders, giving them a detailed understanding of the schools they support. However, the organisation recognises that its current approach to planning and prioritisation relies heavily on this network of relationships and on information that reaches headquarters unevenly. When a new crisis erupts or an existing one escalates, the teams closest to the situation respond first — but the broader picture of how education infrastructure is affected across regions is difficult to assemble quickly.

EBI has recently begun exploring how data and technology could complement its field expertise. A preliminary exercise in one country programme used publicly available data to map school locations against population estimates, revealing significant discrepancies between where schools existed and where the highest concentrations of school-age children lived. The results confirmed that open data, combined with the right analytical tools, could meaningfully strengthen how the organisation plans and prioritises its work. EBI is now seeking external expertise to develop this capacity.

This RFP seeks proposals for a project that would strengthen EBI’s education programming in conflict-affected areas through data-driven approaches.

## 2. Objective

The objective of this RFP is to solicit proposals for strengthening how EBI plans and prioritises its education programming in conflict-affected areas. Proposals should describe how data analysis, geospatial tools, and AI can complement the organisation’s field expertise and improve its decision-making.

EBI is particularly interested in approaches that:
*   Complement rather than replace the knowledge of field teams
*   Use open, publicly available data sources that do not require proprietary licences
*   Produce tools and outputs that non-technical staff can understand and use
*   Can be adapted to new regions or changing conflict dynamics without starting from scratch

## 3. Scope of Work

Proposals should address the following key areas:

*   **Needs assessment:** Propose an approach to better understand the relationship between conflict dynamics and education access in the regions where EBI operates, drawing on available data about conflict events, school locations, population distribution, and any other relevant indicators.
*   **Approach and methodology:** Describe how the proposed project would be carried out, including how different data sources would be combined to support decision-making.
*   **Use of AI:** Indicate how AI tools could inform the work, improve efficiency, or support monitoring and scalability.
*   **Adaptability:** Explain how the proposed approach could be applied to different regions or adapted as conflict dynamics change. EBI operates across multiple country contexts with varying data availability; proposals should consider this diversity.

## 4. Proposal Requirements

Proposals must include the following components:

*   **Strategy:** The context, objectives, proposed approach, activities, and deliverables.
*   **Use of technology:** A description of how AI and other tools would support the proposed approach.

Shortlisted proposers will be invited to submit a detailed proposal including budget, timeline, and implementation plan.

## 5. Evaluation Criteria

Proposals will be evaluated based on the following criteria:

*   **Relevance and innovation:** Alignment with RFP objectives and the quality of the proposed approach.
*   **Data-driven approach:** How effectively the proposal uses data to identify needs and support decisions.
*   **Use of technology:** The clarity and realism of how AI and other tools are integrated into the project.
*   **Feasibility and impact:** The practicality of the proposed approach and the potential impact on EBI’s education programming.
*   **Scalability and adaptability:** The potential for the approach to be applied in other regions or to other aspects of EBI’s work.
